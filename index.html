About Me


Developer and Engineer

Here's what I would look like if I was a Java class

I love computers. Ever since I got my first one in Grade Two (A 2004 IBM ThinkPad on Windows XP!) I've been fascinated by them. Now, I try my best to harness that power into some pretty cool stuff! I do a lot of web development: I've made websites of every kind, including portfolio websites, social media sites, conference websites (for WAC and OMUN), sports league websites, my blog, and a plethora of other projects. I've also developed a conference app, a few games, and a bunch of other things you can find on my GitHub page. I've scored perfect on the Junior CCC, and placed top 8 in our ECOO Regionals. In addition, I'm the head of our school's Robotics Club, teaching kids at UCC the entire engineering workflow starting from prototyping to winning competitions. I also participate in Horizons Robotics, a non-profit organization dedicated to teaching the things that made me passionate about technology to underpriveleged kids all over Toronto. I also moonlight as an A/V associate, doing small work for my school and the World Affairs Conference.

World Affairs Conference Website

The World Affairs Conference is the oldest student-led conference, but that doesn't mean we have to be behind the times. I developed the World Affairs Conference Website, which included remaking the entire structure, content, and some server-side design. Using Jekyll, I've written a website that parses markdown files and turns it into a fully-fledged website, which makes it easily maintainable by people who don't know how to code.

We still have a lot of goals ahead of us, such as online registration, plenary assignments, and delegate outreach. It's a lot of work, but you can watch our progress on the website, as well as on our GitHub repository.

UCC Robotics Website

It'd be nice if robots could build our website for us, but technology hasn't advanced that far. In the meantime, I've developed the UCC Robotics Website, which features tons (and tonnes) of content, a user system that includes messaging, achievements, and attendance, and a dynamic content-delviery system, using alerts and bulletins.

Unfortunately, not all of those features are polished, and there's still a lot of work to do. You can track our progress on the GitHub Repository, and even contribute via issue tracking or through contributing code.

Prefects Cup Website

Much like Hogwarts, UCC features 10 houses filled to the brim with competitive nature. Yet, there wasn't one centralized way to visualize the results of the vast number of competitions such as House Softball, Hockey, or Soccer: just a bunch of Google Spreadsheets, posted flyers, and assembly announcements scattered all over the place. The Prefects Cup Website, in it's own way, tries to fix that. It features a centralized (albeit small) schedule bank, a list of all the organisers, and most importantly, an almost live-update of the position of each house.

You can find the code for the website on GitHub, but I am currently making a version 2. Psssst. Go Orr's go!

Ontario Model UN Website

Model United Nations Conferences are one of my favourite extracurricular events: you spend several days debating key issues that affect the globe, such as world hunger, the Middle East, or even a sudden zombie outbreak. Ontario Model United Nations is held at my school, Upper Canada College, and to help out I've designed the Ontario Model UN Website, which delivers content about the conference such as scheduling, committees, and position papers.

My next big goal is online registration, but that seems like a daunting hurdle. Nevertheless, I'll give it my best shot. You can keep tabs on my progress on the GitHub Repository.

Ontario Model UN App

We're in the digital age, and all the popular conferences have conference apps. At OMUN, we want to stay in the game; that's why I'm working on the Ontario Model UN App. It'll feature similar information as the website, as well as additional news blasts and notifications, all at the tip of your fingers.

The OMUN App is developed using web technologies, so it'll be available on Android and iOS soon. Expect something after summer break! You can keep up on my progress on the GitHub Repository.
