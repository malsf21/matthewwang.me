About Me

Now, I could tell you that I'm an "inspiring public speaker", or an "innovative and disruptive student developer", or a "funny person", but that's boring, and you have no way of knowing if it's true or not (hint, the last one's a lie). Instead, let me tell you what I care about.

I care about education. That's a pretty broad topic, but that means there's a lot to do. I've created and fostered programs like Horizons Robotics, where I lead a team of tutors to give awesome inner-city kids the opportunity to learn awesome things about robotics..

I care about technology. I know, that's pretty stereotypical, but it's true. Modern technology and the internet have a treasure trove of cool things. I try to do make cool things too, like web applications, pretty graphics, and lean machines. Sometimes, they're cool too.

I care about debate. Weird thing to care about, but trust me. It's a great way for me to learn about all the different ways I've been wrong about things in life. Plus, I get to spread the knowledge in my own school's club, and also as a camp counselor at Debate Camp Canada.

In my spare time, I do some blogging, listen to music, read, and play video games. And think of good puns.
