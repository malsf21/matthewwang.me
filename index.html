About Me


I'm a teenager.

I'm Matt, a 15 year old guy living in Toronto, Ontario. I go to Upper Canada College (UCC), and am currently in Foundation Year (the pre-IB equivalent of OSSD Grade 10). I like being involved in communities: I debate, lead our robotics club, Model UN, and a bunch of other little side projects. In my free time, I play League, Counterstrike, and some singleplayer games; you might be able to catch me when I stream. I also enjoy listening to music, the cliche long walk on the beach, and recreational badminton. Though, there's nothing I enjoy more than a good pun!

I'm a student.

Stay in school to me means more than just academics. I'm heavily involved in educational programs, whether it's through leading our school's robotics club, the school newspaper, helping organize events like the World Affairs Conference, or attending programs like DEEP and SHAD. I also don't shy away from competition: I'm part of UCC Debate, UCC Model UN, UCC DECA, Reach for the Top, or even the odd squash or badminton game, and I'm always ready to try something new out (as cliche as that sounds).

I'm a community member.

I've gotten a lot from the community, so I look to give back. Through founding and organizing the Horizons Robotics program, I take the same program that created my love for robotics and bring it to kids all over Toronto. Through Horizons Math, I try to spread my love for math to other students, and hopefully foster that same love.

robotics.ucc.on.ca

It'd be nice if robots could build our website for us, but technology hasn't advanced that far. In the meantime, I've developed the UCC Robotics Website, which features tons (and tonnes) of content, a user system that includes messaging, achievements, and attendance, and a dynamic content-delviery system, using alerts and bulletins.

Unfortunately, not all of those features are polished, and there's still a lot of work to do. You can track our progress on the GitHub Repository, and even contribute via issue tracking or through contributing code.

world.ac

The World Affairs Conference is the oldest student-led conference, but that doesn't mean we have to be behind the times. For WAC 2016, I developed the website, which included remaking the entire structure, content, and some server-side design.

For the next WAC, there's a lot of talk about online registration, as well as making the site more accessible and easy to read. It's a lot of work, but you can watch our progress on the website, as well as my Twitter.

Project ScrumHalf

It's a bit shady to call class work a project, but project ScrumHalf is more than just class work. It's my first iteration of coding a complete game from the ground up, without any of those libraries, online resources, or whatever! Obviously, it's not as great as it could be, but it's a learning experience. Furthermore, it's my first time officially using Agile Scrum, a software development method largely used in the real world. It's nice to work together as a team, with resource managers, sprint backlogs, and scrum masters (that's me!).

You can check on our progress through our FYCompSci GitHub repo, and hopefully witness a transformation into an actually functional game. If you want to play our game, you need to visit this link and also enable ECMA6 in your browser (a quick google search should do).

pc.ucc.on.ca

Much like Hogwarts, UCC features houses filled to the brim with competitive nature. Yet, there wasn't one centralized way to visualize that competitiveness: just a bunch of Google Spreadsheets, posted flyers, and assembly announcements about who won what. The Prefects Cup, in it's own way, tries to fix that. It features a centralized (albeit small) schedule bank, a list of all the organisers, and most importantly, an almost live-update of the position of each house.
